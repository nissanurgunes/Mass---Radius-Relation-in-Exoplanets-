# Mass---Radius-Relation-in-Exoplanets-
Abstract
Exoplanet research is one of the most current and important areas of research in modern astronomy and astrophysics. The study of these planets outside our solar system not only demonstrates how diverse and vast the universe is, but also helps us understand topics such as planetary formation and evolution, planetary types and structures, and potential habitability conditions. This project involved a study of exoplanets that have been confirmed in the literature and for which mass-radius data are available. The data analysed within the scope of our project were examined using Microsoft Excel to determine the relationship between mass and radius. The graphs drawn based on the data obtained were also analysed using this programme. Our analyses revealed a varying mass-radius relationship for different types of exoplanets, resulting in three different equations for small, medium, and large-mass exoplanets. These equations are a crucial tool that allows us to estimate the other quantity when either the mass or radius is available, thereby providing insight into the type and structure of the exoplanet. In this study, functional equations were obtained separately for each class of planets characterised by breakpoints determined using a simpler and more applicable method.
